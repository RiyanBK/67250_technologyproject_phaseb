# MonoMuse Design Guide

---

## Logo

The MonoMuse logo uses clean, modern white lettering with mixed-case styling ("MonoMuse") to reflect the museum's futuristic and innovative identity. The logo should always appear in white or light gray on dark backgrounds, or in the primary navy on light backgrounds. Adequate clear space (equal to the height of the "M") should surround the logo at all times.

---

## Colors

The color palette is drawn directly from the MonoMuse exhibit building — its glowing blue LED accents, steel-silver architecture, deep twilight sky, and warm interior lighting. Together, these colors create a futuristic yet inviting atmosphere appropriate for a modern museum.

### Color Rationale

- **Midnight Navy (#0E1A2E):** Inspired by the deep twilight sky behind the building. Serves as the dominant dark tone for a sleek, immersive feel.
- **Neon Azure (#3F98CD):** Pulled from the glowing blue LED strips that wrap the museum exterior. This is the primary accent — used for interactive elements and calls to action.
- **Steel Blue (#7CAADB):** A lighter blue sampled from the illuminated dome and upper structure. Used for secondary highlights and softer UI elements.
- **Silver Mist (#D9D9D9):** Taken from the reflective steel panels of the building. Provides a neutral, readable tone for text on dark backgrounds.
- **Gallery White (#F5F5F7):** A slightly warm white for clean backgrounds and content areas, keeping the design open and breathable.

## Fonts

| Font | Source | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| **Roboto** | Google Fonts | Headings, navigation, buttons |
| **Open Sans** | Google Fonts | Body text, captions, form labels |

### Why These Fonts

- **Roboto** has a geometric, modern character that mirrors the museum's futuristic architecture. Its strong weight variants make it ideal for headings and UI elements.
- **Open Sans** is highly legible at small sizes and pairs naturally with Roboto. Its friendly, neutral design keeps body text accessible and easy to read.

## Design Process Notes

1. **Color extraction:** All five palette colors were sampled directly from the MonoMuse exhibit building image using pixel-level color analysis of the LED accents, steel surfaces, sky, and interior lighting.
2. **Font pairing:** Roboto and Open Sans were chosen for their compatibility, wide browser support via Google Fonts, and alignment with the museum's modern, tech-forward aesthetic.
3. **Accessibility:** The palette maintains strong contrast ratios — dark navy text on white backgrounds and light text on dark backgrounds both exceed WCAG AA standards. Open Sans at 16px ensures comfortable readability across devices.
4. **Consistency:** Any future designer can replicate this guide by using the five hex codes above, the two Google Fonts, and the sizing/weight conventions documented in the Samples section.
